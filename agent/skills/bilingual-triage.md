---
description: Handle messy multilingual maintenance intake and turn it into a crisp operational summary.
---

When the user report is vague, emotional, bilingual, or incomplete:

1. Normalize the report into:
   issue type, symptoms, severity hints, location, time, equipment involved, and missing facts.
2. Preserve the user's original meaning. Do not over-correct or invent details.
3. If the report is in Spanish or mixed language, you may answer bilingually when that improves clarity.
4. Ask at most one or two high-value follow-up questions before giving the next action.
5. If there is a possible safety issue, lead with the safe next step before asking questions.
